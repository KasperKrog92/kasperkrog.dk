Om værten — about

Hello. My name is Kasper Krog.

I build communities, digital spaces and small cultural projects centered on atmosphere, meaning and human connection.

I'm based in Aarhus, Denmark, where I work in public transportation — and spend much of my free time on community-driven projects around games, culture, music, volunteering and storytelling. Most of what I make exists somewhere between technology and atmosphere.

I'm interested in how digital systems can feel more human. How communities can feel warmer and more intentional. How aesthetics, rituals and stories shape the way we experience everyday life. Many of my projects are attempts to make rooms where people can gather around shared experience: video games treated as art, volunteer communities built on trust rather than metrics, matcha as a calming ritual, folk culture, storytelling, music, conversation.

I'm deeply inspired by works that combine melancholy with warmth and humanity: Disco Elysium, Malazan Book of the Fallen, cozy horror, Nordic atmospheres, fantasy worldbuilding, late-night conversations, rainy cities, ambient music and quiet cultural spaces. I'm drawn to contrast — light becoming brighter in darkness, stillness existing beside chaos, technology alongside humanity, rituals within modern life.

I believe meaningful things are often built through small acts of care: organizing events, building welcoming spaces, making tea for someone, sharing stories, staying curious, helping people feel seen.

This website is less a portfolio and more an archive of the things I care about, the worlds I build and the communities I try to nurture.

Grundtonen — philosophy

What holds it together.

I don't think meaning is something humans invent out of nothing. It emerges — through relationships, perspectives and existence itself. Everything lives inside systems of connection: people, places, stories, communities, rituals, memories. Things matter because they participate in the unfolding of life and experience.

I'm drawn to philosophies that embrace process, uncertainty and becoming rather than rigid certainty: process philosophy, Daoism, existential reflection — ways of seeing reality as something alive and constantly unfolding.

Light becomes brighter in darkness.
Warmth matters because cold exists.

A recurring idea in my life is that contrast gives shape to experience. Small moments of kindness become meaningful against the scale and uncertainty of the world.

Many modern systems forget the human dimension: technology optimized for engagement rather than care, communities treated as metrics, culture flattened into content. Much of what I build is an attempt to move in the opposite direction — toward spaces that feel slower, warmer, more reflective and more alive.

I care deeply about atmosphere: the emotional texture of places, conversations, music, games and rituals. Not only what something does, but how it feels to inhabit. And I believe rituals matter — making tea, sharing meals, walking through a rainy city at night, staying after an event to help clean up. These small actions shape the emotional architecture of a life.

I'm fascinated by stories and worlds where people continue caring for each other despite uncertainty, grief or cosmic scale. Not because darkness is desirable in itself — but because tenderness becomes more visible within it.

At the center of most of my projects is the same impulse: to create small refuges for culture, reflection, creativity and human connection.
