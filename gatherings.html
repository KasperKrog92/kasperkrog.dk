what we gather around.

Projects are one thing. The better question is what they are for: rooms where shared experience can happen, kept warm by the people inside them.

monthly

Gamestormers evenings gamestormers.dk

Once a month, a room in Aarhus fills with people who talk about games the way others talk about films or novels — endings, scenes, what a mechanic made them feel. Newcomers are looked after. The quiet ones are welcome to stay quiet.

games as culture · conversation · belonging

Tabletop & roleplaying

Dice, paper, and the oldest technology we have: strangers becoming a party within the hour. Some of the worlds in the back room were first spoken aloud at these tables.

ttrpg · shared imagination · improvisation

fields

Folk festival folk.gamestormers.dk

A volunteer-driven folk festival is an argument that culture can be intergenerational: someone's grandmother and someone's teenager on the same dance floor. I help where helping is needed.

folk culture · tradition · volunteering

Venues & volunteers turkis.gamestormers.dk

Cultural spaces are carried by volunteers — the people who arrive early and stay past the last song. Turkis Crew exists so the tools they use can feel as humane as the work they do.
